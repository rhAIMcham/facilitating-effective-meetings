Participant type Typical needs Example
Senior executives or decision-makers High-level insights on key issues, decisions and implications. Provide a one-page briefing or executive summary. Focus on implications and next steps.
Technical or subject matter experts Access to detailed data, reports or technical analysis. Share comprehensive documents or data in advance and specify which sections need review.
Operational staff or team members Clarity on practical issues, current progress and next actions. Offer background context and outline expectations for their input during discussions.
Stakeholders or partners Understanding of the meeting’s purpose, context and potential impact. Distribute summary materials that explain the discussion’s relevance and their role in it.
External participants (e.g. clients, contractors) Awareness of organisational background, project scope and objectives. Provide a short orientation or briefing document to align them with internal perspectives.
