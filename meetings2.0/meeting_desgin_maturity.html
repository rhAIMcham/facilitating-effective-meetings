Meeting Design

This area evaluates how effectively an organisation understands and standardises its approach to different types of meetings.

Level 1: Individual
Minimal distinction between meeting types. No standardised approach or guidelines. Meetings are inconsistent in structure, purpose and outcomes.
Level 2: Professional
Expected meeting practices are documented.
Level 3: Effective
Business units use predictable meeting structures and cadences designed to deliver pre-determined business outcomes. Distinct meeting roles are identified and documented. Meeting designs clarify the process for making decisions within that business unit.
Level 4: Systematised
Business process definition includes identification and integration of meeting practices across business units. Standard organisation-wide meeting structures are documented for each distinct type of meeting. Standardised decision-making methods are developed and shared across the organisation.
Level 5: Optimised / World Class
Meeting designs intentionally reflect organisational values and strategic priorities. Existing meeting designs evolve based on experimentation and regular critique. Proactive initiatives are taken to design new meeting structures based upon gaps identified through self-assessments.
Question 2 of 5

Meeting Skills

This focus area assesses the capabilities of participants to schedule, convene, facilitate, design and actively engage in meetings.

Level 1: Individual
Organisation takes no special effort to recruit or cultivate meeting skills. Facilitation is inconsistent and participation varies.
Level 2: Professional
Meeting leaders are expected to follow documented practices. Meeting records provide evidence that the expected meeting practices were followed.
Level 3: Effective
Training programs for meeting leaders in best practice methodology and basic facilitation are in place. Training and/or mentoring on meeting participation is in place. Job descriptions and interviews formally address meeting conduct.
Level 4: Systematised
Training programs for meeting facilitation and design are in place. Meeting leaders and participants receive ongoing training and have access to mentorship and coaching programs. Meeting performance expectations are documented and discussed as part of job performance review.
Level 5: Optimised / World Class
Meeting facilitation and design is a recognised job function and core competency within the organisation. Effective meeting participation enables distributed decision making throughout the organisation.

Stakeholder Satisfaction

This component measures whether meetings are perceived as valuable, productive and relevant by all participants and stakeholders.

Level 1: Individual
Feedback on meeting effectiveness is rarely collected. Participant satisfaction is unknown or ignored.
Level 2: Professional
Meeting participants are empowered to require meetings adhere to documented practices.
Level 3: Effective
Key stakeholders are identified, including both meeting participants and those impacted by meeting outcomes. Metrics are established and collected for both qualitative and quantitative meeting satisfaction and performance indicators. Proactive initiatives are in place to minimise or eliminate ineffective meetings.
Level 4: Systematised
Stakeholder feedback is consistently collected across the organisation. Inappropriate meeting structures or behaviours impacting stakeholder expectations receive corrective attention from management. Meeting performance metrics are available for audit and regularly reviewed.
Level 5: Optimised / World Class
Stakeholder feedback validates that the meeting structures used meet or exceed stakeholder expectations. External stakeholders and observers have qualitatively consistent meeting experiences when meeting with different groups within the organisation.

Facilities, Technology and Resources

This area evaluates the infrastructure that supports effective meetings, including tools, technology and physical spaces.

Level 1: Individual
Basic scheduling support only; facilities and technology are ad-hoc. Little consideration for optimising the participant experience.
Level 2: Professional
Support for scheduling, holding, and recording meeting outcomes is in place.
Level 3: Effective
Centralised meeting records are available for the standard meetings conducted by each unit. Meeting facilities and technology support the designed meeting processes.
Level 4: Systematised
Systems are in place to automate high frequency meeting tasks. Meeting content is centrally available and can be easily reviewed. The organisation maintains a well-stocked meeting toolkit, including appropriate facilities, technology and supplies for in-person meetings and remote meetings.
Level 5: Optimised / World Class
Meeting facilities and technology are consistently updated to support a wide range of meeting practices. Individuals throughout the organization receive support for participation in professional development activities outside the organisation.
Question 5 of 5

Cultural Ownership

Cultural ownership reflects the organisation's commitment to consistent, values-driven meeting practices.

Level 1: Individual
Meeting practices vary widely between managers or teams. Norms are informal and inconsistent, with limited accountability.
Level 2: Professional
Meeting expectations are consistently communicated across business units.
Level 3: Effective
Participation from everyone invited to a meeting is expected, encouraged, and supported by the meeting structures in use. Updated meeting practices are developed with input from various employee groups. Employees feel empowered to raise questions about meeting practices and performance.
Level 4: Systematised
Meetings incorporate organisation specific language and rituals that harmonise the organisational culture across business units. Employees and leaders assume joint ownership for meeting success. People are recognised for demonstrating effective meeting skills.
Level 5: Optimised / World Class
Meetings embody the organisation's declared culture. Meeting design efforts optimise for both business outcomes and cultural cohesion. Past meeting outcomes are reviewed and evaluated for net positive impact.
